## The GAMET Framework — 5 Evaluation Criteria

### G — Guidance (Does the system lead the user?)

- Does the system show the user what to do next?
- Is the most important action visually dominant?
- Are next steps suggested, not just available?

### A — Adoption (Would this role CHOOSE to use this?)

- What does the user gain vs their current method (email/phone/Excel/paper)?
- Is the friction low enough that they come back voluntarily?
- **Predict:** Adoption probability (%) with risk factors

### M — Meaning (Value impossible without the system)

- What insight does the user get that they couldn't calculate themselves?
- Does the system aggregate, analyze, or predict — not just display?

### E — Ease (First-timer understands in 2 minutes)

- Could someone figure this out without training?
- Is language plain and jargon-free?
- **Day One test:** First login — would they understand?
- **Return test:** After 3 months away, can they re-orient?

### T — Trust (Does the user trust the system?)

- Does the system show WHERE data comes from?
- Can the user VERIFY before committing?
- Does the system acknowledge uncertainty?

## Grade Calibration

- **A**: Best-in-class among {{INDUSTRY}} systems. Users actively praise it.
- **B**: Solid professional quality. No complaints.
- **C**: Functional but mediocre. Users tolerate it.
- **D**: Below standard. Users work around it.
- **F**: Broken or harmful to adoption.

## Review Format

Each panelist provides:

1. **Grade (A-F)** calibrated per above
2. **GAMET scores** — one sentence per criterion
3. **Error state assessment** — what happens when things go wrong?
4. **One must-change** with business impact
5. **Dream improvement**
